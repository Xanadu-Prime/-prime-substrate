— Decision Stability Rule 1
DSR‑1 — Declaration → Scope Binding → Razor Stabilization
1. Rule Identifier
DSR‑1 — The First Decision Stability Rule of the Xi/Razor Governance Model.

2. Intent
To ensure that every decision entering the substrate is:

explicitly declared

bound to a defined scope

stabilized through the Razor

and only then allowed to propagate

DSR‑1 prevents drift, ambiguity, and silent mutation at the foundation layer.

3. Formal Statement
A decision may not enter the substrate unless it has been:

Declared by the operator

Bound to an explicit and finite scope

Stabilized through the Xi/Razor contradiction‑resolution process

Only after these three conditions are satisfied may the decision propagate into the substrate or any dependent layer.

4. Definitions
Decision — any operator‑initiated change affecting structure, naming, governance, or lifecycle.

Declaration — the operator’s explicit statement of intent.

Scope Binding — the definition of what the decision affects and what it does not.

Razor Stabilization — contradiction resolution via Reconcile, Split, or Elevate.

Propagation — the act of making the decision part of the substrate’s stable state.

5. Preconditions
Before DSR‑1 can be applied:

the operator must articulate the decision in declarative form

the affected surfaces must be known

any conflicting prior decisions must be visible

lineage for the affected artifacts must be intact

If any precondition fails, the decision cannot proceed.

6. Mechanics
6.1 Declaration
The operator states the decision in a single, unambiguous sentence.

6.2 Scope Binding
The operator defines:

the surfaces affected

the surfaces explicitly not affected

the boundaries of the change

the expected lineage impact

6.3 Razor Stabilization
The Razor evaluates contradictions and resolves them via:

Reconcile — unify

Split — separate

Elevate — move to a higher abstraction

Only after stabilization is the decision considered safe.

7. Postconditions
After DSR‑1 completes:

the decision has a stable, contradiction‑free form

the scope is fixed and cannot drift

lineage is updated

the decision is ready for propagation

No further changes may be made without re‑entering DSR‑1.

8. Invariant
A decision that has not passed DSR‑1 does not exist in the substrate.

9. Examples
Example 1 — Adding a new directory
Declare: “Add 02_DOCTRINE directory.”

Bind scope: affects only top‑level structure; no changes to governance.

Stabilize: Razor confirms no naming conflict.

Propagate: directory created.

Example 2 — Renaming a file
Declare: “Rename substrate-spec.md to substrate-specification.md.”

Bind scope: affects naming only.

Stabilize: Razor detects conflict with naming conventions → operator revises.

Propagate: rename applied.
