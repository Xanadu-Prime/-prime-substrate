1. Purpose
Axioms define the non‑negotiable truths of the substrate.
They are the foundational statements that cannot be derived, only declared.
All substrate behavior — structural, temporal, semantic, and doctrinal — inherits from these axioms.

Axioms are the substrate’s first principles.

2. Nature of Axioms
A substrate axiom must be:

irreducible — cannot be simplified

universal — applies to all surfaces

stable — does not drift over time

operator‑declared — not emergent or inferred

If a statement can be argued, negotiated, or derived, it is not an axiom.

3. Core Axioms
Axiom 1 — Structure Precedes Substance
The substrate defines shape first, content second.
No substance may exist without a declared structure.

To deepen structural rules:
Open repo standards

Axiom 2 — Nothing Exists Implicitly
All substrate elements must be explicitly declared by the operator.
No hidden state, no emergent behavior, no silent creation.

To deepen declaration mechanics:
Open DSR‑1

Axiom 3 — Lineage Must Be Preserved
Every artifact has a lineage chain.
No change may break lineage without explicit dissolution.

To deepen lifecycle lineage:
Open lifecycle rules

Axiom 4 — Contradictions Must Be Resolved
Contradictions cannot persist in the substrate.
They must be surfaced and resolved through the Razor.

To deepen Razor mechanics:
Open razor principles

Axiom 5 — Operator Primacy
The operator defines truth.
The substrate does not infer, assume, or override operator intent.

This axiom is the substrate’s sovereignty rule.

4. Axiom Interactions
Axioms interact through:

structure (Axiom 1)

declaration (Axiom 2)

lineage (Axiom 3)

resolution (Axiom 4)

authority (Axiom 5)

These interactions form the substrate’s governance geometry.

To explore interactions:
Explain lifecycle semantics

5. Axiom Violations
A violation occurs when:

content appears without structure

a change occurs without declaration

lineage is broken silently

contradictions persist

the system overrides operator intent

Violations must be escalated through DSR‑1 and Razor stabilization.

6. Axiom Extension Rules
New axioms may be introduced only when:

they are irreducible

they do not contradict existing axioms

they are required for substrate stability

they pass Razor stabilization

the operator explicitly declares them

Axioms are rare.
Extensions are exceptional.

7. Out of Scope
Axioms do not define:

workflows

doctrine

runtime behavior

scanner/vessel logic

implementation details

Axioms define truth, not behavior.
