Razor Principles — Prime Governance Layer (v1.0)
1. Purpose
The Razor is the contradiction engine of the Prime substrate.
It provides the Operator with a deterministic method for:

surfacing contradictions

classifying contradictions

resolving contradictions

stabilizing decisions

preventing drift

The Razor is not philosophy.
The Razor is a mechanism.

To deepen governance:
DSR‑1

2. Razor Definition
The Razor is the system that enforces:

explicitness

coherence

lineage preservation

non‑retroactivity

structural clarity

It ensures that no contradiction can silently persist in the substrate.

To deepen substrate rules:
Substrate Axioms

3. Razor Workflow
All contradictions pass through a deterministic sequence:

Surface — contradictions must be made explicit

Classify — determine the contradiction type

Resolve — apply one of the three Razor outcomes

Stabilize — produce a contradiction‑free form

Propagate — allow the decision to enter lineage

This workflow is the backbone of Prime governance.

4. Types of Contradictions
The Razor recognizes three classes:

4.1 Structural Contradictions
Conflicts in shape, hierarchy, or placement.

4.2 Semantic Contradictions
Conflicts in meaning, naming, or intent.

4.3 Temporal Contradictions
Conflicts in lifecycle, ordering, or propagation.

Each class requires a different stabilization strategy.

To deepen lifecycle:
Lifecycle Rules

5. The Three Razor Outcomes
Every contradiction resolves through one of three outcomes:

5.1 Reconcile
Two competing interpretations are unified into a single coherent form.
Used when both sides describe the same underlying concept.

Explain Reconcile

5.2 Split
The contradiction reveals two distinct concepts that must be separated.
Used when the conflict indicates category confusion.

Explain Split

5.3 Elevate
The contradiction indicates a higher‑order abstraction that resolves both sides.
Used when neither side is wrong — they are incomplete.

Explain Elevate

6. Razor Invariants
The Razor enforces:

No silent contradictions

No implicit stabilization

No propagation without resolution

No drift after stabilization

No retroactive reinterpretation

These invariants ensure the substrate remains coherent and deterministic.

7. Operator Role
The Operator is responsible for:

declaring contradictions

selecting the correct Razor outcome

validating stabilization

authorizing propagation

The Razor does not decide.
The Operator decides through the Razor.

To deepen Operator doctrine:
Operator Doctrine

8. Razor–DSR‑1 Integration
The Razor is invoked during the Stabilize phase of DSR‑1:

Declare

Scope‑Bind

Razor‑Stabilize

Propagate

No decision may propagate without Razor stabilization.

9. Razor Failure Modes
A Razor failure occurs when:

contradictions are not surfaced

contradictions are misclassified

stabilization is incomplete

propagation occurs prematurely

lineage is broken

Failures must be escalated through a new DSR‑1 cycle.

10. Out of Scope
The Razor does not define:

substrate axioms

lifecycle semantics

naming conventions

runtime behavior

public surfaces

The Razor governs contradictions, not content.
