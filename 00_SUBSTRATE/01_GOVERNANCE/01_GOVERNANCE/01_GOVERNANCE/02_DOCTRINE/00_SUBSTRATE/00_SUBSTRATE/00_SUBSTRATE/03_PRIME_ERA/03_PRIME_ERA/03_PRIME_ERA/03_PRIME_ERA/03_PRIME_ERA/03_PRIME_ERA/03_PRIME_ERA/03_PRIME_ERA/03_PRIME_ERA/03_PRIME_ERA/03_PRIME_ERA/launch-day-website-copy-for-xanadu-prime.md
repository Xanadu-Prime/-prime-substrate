Xanadu Prime
The first operational instance of the Xanadu architecture
Xanadu Prime marks the moment the hypertext vision becomes real — a governed, stable, end‑to‑end system built on permanent addresses, deep versioning, true transclusion, and bidirectional links.

Prime is the origin point of the modern Docuverse.

What Is Xanadu Prime?
Xanadu Prime is a structurally complete hypertext substrate.
It provides:

permanent, stable addressing

immutable deep versioning

reference‑based transclusion

bidirectional links

governed lineage

Prime is the first system to implement the full architecture as a functioning whole.

Why Prime Matters
For decades, hypertext systems have been copy‑based, mutable, and fragile.
Prime introduces a substrate where:

nothing is overwritten

every version is preserved

every link is stable

every transclusion is authentic

every change is governed

This is the foundation of a permanent, interconnected document universe.

run end‑to‑end

Core Capabilities
Stable Addressing
Every element — document, version, span — has a permanent address.

Deep Versioning

True Transclusion

Bidirectional Links

How Prime Works
Prime is built on three layers:

1. Substrate Layer
Defines existence: axioms, lifecycle, addressing, transclusion, lineage.

2. Governance Layer
Ensures stability: DSR‑1, Razor, naming rules, invariants.

3. Operational Layer
Implements behavior: documents, links, versions, public surfaces.
