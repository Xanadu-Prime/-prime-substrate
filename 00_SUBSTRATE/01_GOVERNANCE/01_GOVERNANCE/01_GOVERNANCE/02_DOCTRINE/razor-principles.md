1. Purpose
The Razor defines the contradiction engine of the substrate.
It provides the operator with a deterministic method for:

identifying contradictions

resolving them

stabilizing decisions

ensuring structural clarity

The Razor is not a philosophy.
It is a mechanism.

2. Core Principle
The Razor asserts:

Contradictions must be surfaced, resolved, and stabilized before any decision may propagate.

This principle governs all substrate evolution.

3. Razor Functions
The Razor performs three primary functions:

Detection — reveal contradictions

Evaluation — classify contradictions

Resolution — apply one of the three Razor outcomes

To deepen Razor mechanics:
Open DSR‑1

4. The Three Razor Outcomes
Every contradiction resolves through one of three outcomes:

4.1 Reconcile
Two competing interpretations are unified into a single, coherent form.

4.2 Split
The contradiction reveals two distinct concepts that must be separated.

4.3 Elevate
The contradiction indicates a higher‑order abstraction that resolves both sides.

To expand each outcome:

Reconcile

Split

Elevate

5. Razor Workflow
The Razor operates through a deterministic sequence:

Surface — contradictions are made explicit

Classify — determine the type of contradiction

Resolve — apply Reconcile, Split, or Elevate

Stabilize — produce a contradiction‑free form

Propagate — allow the decision to enter the substrate

This workflow is the backbone of DSR‑1.

6. Types of Contradictions
The Razor recognizes three classes:

Structural contradictions — shape, hierarchy, lineage

Semantic contradictions — meaning, naming, intent

Temporal contradictions — lifecycle, ordering, propagation

Each class requires a different stabilization strategy.

To deepen lifecycle contradictions:
Open lifecycle rules

7. Operator Role
The operator is responsible for:

declaring contradictions

selecting the correct Razor outcome

validating stabilization

authorizing propagation

The Razor does not decide —
the operator decides through the Razor.

8. Razor Invariants
The Razor enforces:

no silent contradictions

no implicit stabilization

no propagation without resolution

no drift after stabilization

These invariants maintain substrate integrity.

9. Out of Scope
The Razor does not define:

operator doctrine

runtime behavior

scanner/vessel logic

implementation details

It governs clarity, not execution.
